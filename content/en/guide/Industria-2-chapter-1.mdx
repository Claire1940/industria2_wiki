## Early Inventory Setup (Do This Immediately)

The inventory tutorial is short, but the chapter assumes you understood it perfectly. Don’t skim it.

### How the bag is functionally split

| Inventory Segment | What It Does | Best Early Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Stored slots (top)** | Holds items not instantly usable | Keep backup meds and spare utility |
| **Active slots (bottom)** | Items accessible during play | Bandage + ammo + primary utility |
| **Weapon side** | Firearms and related gear | Keep pistol ready and uncluttered |
| **Tool side** | Interaction/support items | Place puzzle-relevant items here |

When Marian gives you access to supplies and the bandage, move healing into active slots right away. That sounds basic, but in **Industria 2 chapter 1**, damage spikes often happen just after traversal moments, when players forget to prep.

### Recommended first-loadout logic

1. Put the bandage into an active slot.
2. Keep one active slot empty for incoming objective items.
3. Avoid hoarding junk in active space.
4. Keep pistol ammo visible so you can track scarcity.

> **💡 Tip:** In early **Industria 2 chapter 1** combat, your biggest advantage is fast recovery and clean slot access—not raw firepower.

## Crafting and Ammo Economy in Chapter 1

You receive the pistol early, but bullets are your real bottleneck. The chapter explicitly introduces crafting with a basic pistol ammo recipe.

### Core recipe you must remember

| Craftable | Required Materials | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Pistol ammo** | **2 metal scraps + 2 gunpowder** | Primary ranged damage in early fights |

This seems cheap, but materials are limited in the immediate route. That means every missed shot has direct opportunity cost. If you enter the Atlas cluster section with low ammo, you’ll be forced into risky close encounters.

### Practical ammo management rules

| Situation | Recommended Action | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Single Hollow at medium range | Aim for exposed weak zones/tank area | Better damage efficiency |
| Two or more hostiles | Reposition before firing | Prevents panic mag-dumps |
| Narrow corridors | Hold angle, don’t chase | Conserves rounds and health |
| After each fight | Loot first, craft second | Prevents overcrafting and waste |

A lot of players ask whether to craft as soon as they can. In **Industria 2 chapter 1**, crafting immediately is fine when you’re below combat comfort threshold, but avoid converting every material instantly. Keep flexibility for future recipes introduced in later chapters.

## Combat Guide: Hollows, Positioning, and Survival Rhythm

Marian’s explanation of Atlas enemies gives you tactical clues: Hollows are machine units powered by Petraore, with visible tanks that sustain their activity. Use that information actively.

### Hollow engagement priorities

| Priority | Target/Action | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Identify nearest flanker | Prevents surround pressure |
| **2** | Aim at vulnerable mechanical sections | Quicker takedowns |
| **3** | Move after short burst | Lowers return-hit chance |
| **4** | Recheck ammo and heal status | Stabilizes next engagement |

### Common mistakes in Chapter 1 combat

- Standing still after landing the first hit.
- Shooting from open ground instead of hard cover edges.
- Overcommitting to finish one Hollow while a second closes in.
- Ignoring environmental movement routes created by debris and structures.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you feel “underpowered,” it is usually a positioning issue, not a weapon issue. In **Industria 2 chapter 1**, bad angles drain your ammo faster than enemy durability does.

### Fast combat loop to practice

1. Spot enemy movement pattern.
2. Back into partial cover.
3. Fire controlled shots.
4. Strafe and reset line of sight.
5. Loot immediately after the area is safe.

That rhythm keeps you alive through the factory-adjacent path and the cluster detour where pressure ramps up.

## FAQ

**Q: How long does Industria 2 chapter 1 usually take?**

A: Most players clear it in about 45–90 minutes depending on exploration depth and combat caution. If you loot thoroughly and learn enemy patterns, expect the longer end on a first run.

**Q: What is the most important resource in Industria 2 chapter 1?**

A: Early on, pistol ammo efficiency is the top priority because it depends on scarce crafting materials. Health management matters too, but poor shooting discipline creates most early failures.

**Q: Should I craft ammo every time I find materials?**

A: Not necessarily. Craft when your current ammo drops below your comfort level for the next engagement. Keeping some raw materials gives flexibility if a new recipe appears soon after.

**Q: Is Chapter 1 mainly story or gameplay-focused?**

A: It’s both. The narrative sets up Nora, Marian, Walter, and Atlas stakes, while gameplay introduces the core survival loop: slot management, crafting decisions, and tactical fights against Hollows.
